Encounter Page CRSC Only
Walk-in client intake and outreach service tracking
‚ñæ
üè¢
CRSC Staff: This section is specific to your role. Before creating an application, you must complete the Encounter page for all walk-in clients. This is how DFSS tracks the full universe of services your team provides ‚Äî even when a client does not enroll or get placed.

As a CRSC, you serve many walk-in clients. The Encounter page (Outreach Service page) allows you to document every interaction ‚Äî regardless of whether the client moves forward to enrollment. DFSS requires this data to understand the complete scope of your team's work.

When to Use the Encounter Page

  • Every walk-in client, even if no services are provided that day
  • Phone contacts and outreach interactions
  • Clients you are assessing for potential workforce referral
  • Clients who are not ready for enrollment but may return later

Step 1 ‚Äî Navigate to the Outreach Service Page

1
Select "Outreach Service" from the left navigation

Found under the Services dropdown on the top left of the page.

2
Search for the client using the Look-Up bar

Enter first name and last name, then click Search & Add.

3
Select or create the client account

If a match exists, verify using DOB, SSN, or Address and click Select. If no match, click Create New Account.

4
Click "+ ADD" next to Outreach Services Tracking

This opens the service entry form.

5
Select services provided and add a comment

If no services were provided yet, select "No Services Provided ‚Äì Only Inquiry." Always fill in the comment section with notes about the interaction.

‚ö†Ô∏è Continue adding to this page even after enrollment or placement.

Referring a Client to a Workforce Agency CRSC Only
How to refer clients from the Encounter page
‚ñæ

When a walk-in client is seeking employment, CRSCs can refer them to a Workforce Agency directly through the Encounter page. Once the referral is made, the receiving agency takes over the Workforce process. CRSCs can also refer clients to themselves if their site provides workforce services.

üìû
After making a referral: ECM will display the referred agency's phone number. Give the agency a courtesy call to let them know a client has been referred and to expect them. This warm handoff improves client follow-through and is a best practice.

Making a Referral

1
Complete the Encounter page entry first

Document the services provided (or "No Services Provided") before initiating a referral.

2
Locate the Referral option on the Encounter page

Select a Workforce Agency to refer the client to.

3
Select the Workforce Agency

Choose the appropriate agency from the referral list. If referring to your own site, select your CRSC agency.

4
Note the agency's phone number displayed

Call the agency to give them a heads-up about the referred client.

üìû This phone call is not required in ECM but is a strongly recommended best practice.

5
Save the referral and continue monitoring via the Encounter page

You can continue adding notes about follow-up contacts with the client even after the referral.

‚úÖ
Referring to yourself? If your CRSC site provides Workforce services, you can refer the client to your own agency. Once complete, proceed through the full Workforce process (Sections 3‚Äì8 below).

Finding or Creating a Client Account
Deduplication ‚Äî always search before creating
‚ñæ
üö®
Always search before creating. Duplicate client records cause reporting errors and can negatively affect a client's service history across the system.

Before creating any new client record, you must search for an existing account. Navigate to the Outreach Service page from the left navigation, then use the Look-Up bar to search.

Searching for a Client

1
Enter First Name and Last Name in the Look-Up bar

Click Search & Add to initiate the search.

2
Review all results carefully

Confirm DOB, SSN (last 4), and Address. A client may appear under a slightly different name spelling.

3
If a match is found ‚Äî click Select

Open the existing record. Do not create a new account.

4
If no match is found ‚Äî click Create New Account

Enter: First Name, Last Name, DOB, SSN (if available), Email or Mobile. If contact info unavailable, select "Account Email or Mobile Number is not Available," then click Sign Up.

Setting Goals & the Individual Employment Plan (IEP)
WF Placement page ‚Äî Goals, IEP, and Progress Notes
‚ñæ

After verifying the application, navigate to the WF Placement page. The three required tabs are: Goals, IEP, and Progress Note.

WF Placement Page Tabs

  • Goals ‚Äî Set employment goals, readiness challenges, barriers, and development targets (Required)
  • IEP (Individual Employment Plan) ‚Äî Track tasks, enroll, employ, and track retention (Required)
  • Progress Note ‚Äî Document all client interaction notes (Required)
  • Work History ‚Äî Past employment (feeds into Resume tab)
  • Education ‚Äî Diplomas, certifications, test results (feeds into Resume tab)
  • Services ‚Äî Domain-specific services for reporting
  • Resume ‚Äî Auto-populated from Work History and Education
  • Document ‚Äî File uploads

Adding a Goal

1
On the Goals tab, select the edit pencil

This opens the Goals form.

2
Complete: Goal Name, Industry, Placement Program

Selecting the Placement Program reveals three additional sections.

3
Select from Readiness Challenges, Employment Barriers, Required Development

Readiness Challenges = areas for improvement. Employment Barriers = barriers to employment. Required Development = certifications/licenses needed.

4
To add a custom Target, click the + icon next to Required Development

Enter the target name, check "Certification" if applicable, then Save.

2

Examples: "Create a Resume," "Interview Practice," "Apply for Jobs."

3
On the Progress Note tab, document all notes related to each task

Notes should be entered regularly to reflect ongoing client activity.

Enrolling and Employing a Client
These actions trigger your performance contract milestones
‚ñæ
üìä
Performance Contract: Enrollment is one of your two key contract milestones. A client cannot be enrolled without at least one IEP task. Timely enrollment entries directly impact your agency's reimbursements.

Enrolling a Client

1
Ensure at least one task exists for each IEP target

ECM requires at least one task before you can enroll.

2
Click Enroll on the IEP tab

Adjust the Enrollment Date if needed, then Save.

3
Continue adding Progress Notes and updating task statuses

As tasks are completed, edit each task, set Task Status to "Completed," then Save.

Employing a Client

‚ö†Ô∏è
Before clicking Employ: update all Pre-Employment notes and task statuses. Once you click Employ, all Pre-Employment tasks are locked and cannot be edited.
1
Click Employ on the IEP tab ‚Üí click OK on the confirmation pop-up

2
Complete all required fields in the Employ pop-up

Industry, Company Name, Placement Date (date job was offered), Work Start Date (first day on the job), Hours per Week, Hourly Rate, Address, Responsibility.

3
Click Save

ECM automatically generates Retention 30, 90, and 180 milestone dates based on the Work Start Date.

üìÖ Work Start Date drives all retention dates. Enter it accurately ‚Äî it cannot be changed after saving.

7
Tracking Job Retention
Retention 30, 90, and 180-day milestones
‚ñæ
üìä
Performance Contract: Retention 30 is your second key billing milestone. Once a client reaches 30 days of employment, you can submit a Retention voucher and unlock that reimbursement.

After employing a client, a Follow-up target is automatically generated on the IEP tab with three default tasks: Retention 30, Retention 90, and Retention 180. ECM calculates these dates automatically from the Work Start Date.

Completing Retention Milestones

1
On the Progress Note tab, document client check-ins at or before each milestone

Note any job updates, challenges, or changes to employment status.

2
Once the client reaches the milestone date, go to IEP ‚Üí Follow-up target

Select the edit pencil next to the milestone task (e.g., Retention 30).

3
Set Task Status to "Completed" and Save

Task Status cannot be set to Completed until the milestone date has passed.

‚ö†Ô∏è After marking Retention 30 as Completed, proceed immediately to Auto Payment Billing to submit your voucher.

Auto Payment Billing (Vouchers) Most Support Calls
Creating, submitting, and tracking voucher payments
‚ñæ
üí≥
This section is critical. Auto Payment Billing is how your agency gets reimbursed for enrollments and Retention 30 completions. Errors or missed steps here are the most common cause of delayed or denied payments. Read every step carefully.

The Auto Payment page has two tabs: Auto Payment (your agency creates and submits vouchers) and Auto Reconciliation (the City approves or denies payments). You will work in the Auto Payment tab.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
A client must have a completed Enrollment or Retention 30 in their WF Placement record before they appear in the billing list. If a client isn't showing up, check their IEP status first.

Creating a Voucher

1
Navigate to the Auto Payment Billing page

Select it from the main left-side navigation.

2
Click CREATE ‚Üí select your Agency ‚Üí click Search

All eligible clients (enrollment and Retention 30) appear in the Billing List.

3
Enter a Voucher Name

Use a clear, consistent naming convention (e.g., "Agency Name ‚Äì Month ‚Äì Type").

4
Select which clients to include using the checkboxes

Only include clients whose records are fully complete and verified.

5
Click Save and Close

The voucher now appears on the Voucher List page.

6
Click Submit to send for City review

Status changes from New ‚Üí Pending.

‚ö†Ô∏è You can only edit a voucher BEFORE submitting. Once submitted, it cannot be changed.

7
Print the Voucher Transmittal Form

After submitting: click Forms ‚Üí Application Forms ‚Üí Voucher Transmittal Form ‚Üí Print.

üö® Common Billing Issues & Troubleshooting

Client isn't appearing in my Billing List when I click Search‚ñæ

The most common cause is that the client's WF Placement record is not complete. Check the following:

‚Ä¢ For Enrollment vouchers: verify the client has been Enrolled in the IEP tab (Enroll button clicked, date saved).

‚Ä¢ For Retention 30 vouchers: verify the Retention 30 task status has been set to "Completed" and saved in IEP ‚Üí Follow-up target.

‚Ä¢ Confirm the client's application is Verified (HH Members tab ‚Üí lock icon shows as locked).

My voucher was Denied ‚Äî what do I do?‚ñæ

Click the History button to find the reason. Common reasons include missing documentation, client not exited from a previous agency, or retention milestone date not yet reached.

After correcting the underlying issue, contact ECM Technical Support: ecm_support@wegrp.com | (844) 726-2999.

I submitted a voucher but need to make a change ‚Äî can I edit it?‚ñæ

No ‚Äî once a voucher is submitted, it cannot be edited. Review your Billing List carefully before clicking Submit.

If submitted in error, contact ECM Technical Support immediately: ecm_support@wegrp.com | (844) 726-2999.

I can't find an old voucher ‚Äî how do I search for it?‚ñæ

Use the AutoPayment Voucher Quick Access menu. Select your Agency, select a Status, and click Search. Click any matching record to open it on the Auto Payment tab.

How do I print the Voucher Transmittal Form?‚ñæ

After submitting: click Forms at the top right ‚Üí click "Application Forms" ‚Üí select print next to Voucher Transmittal Form in the Application Report List.

The Retention 30 task won't let me mark it as Completed‚ñæ

Task Status cannot be set to Completed until the milestone date has actually been reached. Check the Target Date on the Retention 30 task ‚Äî you must wait until that date or after.

If the date has passed and you're still unable to save, verify the Work Start Date entered during the Employ step is correct, as all retention dates are calculated from it.
