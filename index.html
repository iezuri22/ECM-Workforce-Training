The Encounter Page CRSC Only
Documenting services provided to clients in ECM
üèõ
CRSC Staff: This section is specific to your role. The Encounter page is where you document every service interaction with returning citizens. It must be completed for each client contact before a Workforce referral can be made in ECM.

As a CRSC (Community Re-Entry Support Center), you serve returning citizens who come to you through referrals from other agencies and partners, scheduled sessions, phone calls, and walk-ins. Every service interaction is recorded on the Encounter page. This is how DFSS tracks the full scope of services your site provides.

Creating a New Encounter

1
Navigate to the Encounter page and click Add

This opens a new encounter entry form.

2
Select the Method

Choose how the client contacted or came to your site: Walk-in, Call-in, Email, Voicemail, Scheduled Session, or Mail.

3
Confirm the Location of your office

Verify the correct site location is selected.

4
Enter the client's Name and Date of Birth

This identifies the client record tied to this encounter.

5
Enter the client's Address

A valid Chicago address is required. This address will be used to populate nearby Workforce agencies if a referral is needed.‚ö†Ô∏è The address must be a valid Chicago address ‚Äî no address or an invalid one will prevent Workforce agencies from appearing in the referral pop-up.

6
Under Encounter Type, select "Workforce Re-Entry Encounter" from the dropdown
7
Click Save to create the encounter record

The form pop-up will close and you will land on the Encounter page for that client. The edit pencils for Services and Assessment are now available on this page.

‚ö†Ô∏è You must Save first before you can access Services or Assessment. These options are not available until the encounter is saved.
8
Click the edit pencil next to Services to record services provided

Select all applicable services from the checkbox list, then save.

9
Click the edit pencil next to Assessment to complete the client assessment

Complete all 25 required questions. Complete this for every client ‚Äî DFSS uses this data for reporting, and Workforce agencies rely on it when working with the client.

üìã Required: The assessment must be completed and saved before a Workforce referral can be submitted.

Making a Workforce Referral from the Encounter Page

1
Confirm the client's valid Chicago address is entered in the Encounter form

ECM uses this address to locate nearby Workforce agencies. Without a valid Chicago address, no agencies will appear in the pop-up.

Choose the agency best suited for the client based on proximity and available services. CRSCs may also refer to their own agency if Workforce services are offered at their site.

6

Let the agency know a referral is on the way and the client's name.üìû This call is not tracked in ECM but is strongly recommended ‚Äî it significantly improves client follow-through after a referral.

Setting Goals & the Individual Employment Plan (IEP)
WF Placement page ‚Äî the three required tabs

After verifying the application, navigate to the WF Placement page. This is where the entire employment journey is documented. Three tabs are required: Goals, IEP, and Progress Note.

WF Placement ‚Äî 8 Tabs

  • Goals ‚Äî Employment goals, readiness challenges, barriers, development targets (Required)
  • IEP (Individual Employment Plan) ‚Äî Tasks, enrollment, employment, retention (Required)
  • Progress Note ‚Äî All client interaction notes (Required)
  • Work History ‚Äî Past employment; auto-feeds the Resume tab
  • Education ‚Äî Diplomas, certifications, test results; auto-feeds the Resume tab
  • Services ‚Äî Domain-specific services for DFSS reporting
  • Resume ‚Äî Auto-populated from Work History and Education
  • Document ‚Äî File uploads: certificates, case notes, etc.

Adding a Goal

1
Goals tab ‚Üí select the edit pencil to open the Goals form

2
Complete Goal Name, Industry, and Placement Program

Selecting the Placement Program reveals three additional sections.

3
Select from Readiness Challenges, Employment Barriers, and Required Development

Readiness Challenges = areas for improvement. Employment Barriers = barriers to employment. Required Development = certifications or licenses needed.

4
To add a custom Target: click the + icon next to Required Development, enter the name, and Save

Adding IEP Tasks

Each Target has its own IEP. The Placement/Pre-Employment target is present by default for every client.

1
IEP tab ‚Üí select the Pre-Employment target tile (shaded blue when active)

2
Click ADD ‚Üí enter task name and optional description ‚Üí Save

Examples: "Create a Resume," "Interview Practice," "Apply for Jobs."

3
Document all notes on the Progress Note tab regularly

Keeping the IEP Updated Over Time

The IEP is a living record ‚Äî it should be updated every time you meet with or check in on the client. Case managers are expected to keep task statuses current and document activity regularly.

  • Completing a task: Click the edit pencil next to the task ‚Üí set Task Status to Completed ‚Üí enter a Completion Date ‚Üí Save. Do not mark tasks complete before the work is actually done.
  • Adding new tasks: If the client's plan changes or new goals are added, return to the IEP tab and add tasks under the appropriate target tile at any time.
  • Progress Notes: Use the Progress Note tab to record every client interaction ‚Äî phone calls, meetings, check-ins, job applications submitted, employer contacts. These notes are how DFSS auditors and supervisors verify that active case management is happening. Write notes in plain language and include dates.
  • Updating task notes: Each task has its own note field. Use it to log specific activity related to that task (e.g., "Client submitted 3 job applications this week").
‚ö†Ô∏è
Incomplete IEPs cause billing problems. If a client's IEP does not show active tasks and regular progress notes, vouchers may be denied during City review. Keep all records current.

OJT Tab ‚Äî On-the-Job Training

‚ÑπÔ∏è
OJT applies only to Transitional Jobs placement programs. If your agency is not operating under a Transitional Jobs program, this tab does not apply to you.

For agencies running a Transitional Jobs program, the OJT (On-the-Job Training) tab on the WF Placement page is where you document the client's subsidized work experience. This tab tracks the employer, OJT start and end dates, hours worked, and wage reimbursement details.

  • OJT records must be entered before the client can be moved to an Employ record under the Transitional Jobs program.
  • Coordinate with your ECM Administrator if you are unsure whether your program requires OJT documentation.
  • All standard IEP tasks and Progress Notes still apply alongside the OJT tab.

Enrolling and Employing a Client
These actions trigger your performance contract milestones
üìä
Performance Contract: Enrollment is one of your two key reimbursement milestones. ECM requires at least one IEP task before a client can be enrolled ‚Äî this confirms you have an active plan for the client. Timely enrollment entries directly impact your agency's payments.

Enrolling a Client

1
Ensure at least one task is saved for each IEP target

ECM blocks enrollment until at least one task exists.

2

Continue adding Progress Notes and marking tasks as Completed as they are finished

Industry, Company Name, Placement Date (date job was offered), Work Start Date (first day on the job), Hours per Week, Hourly Rate, Address, Responsibilities.

All dates are calculated from the Work Start Date.üìÖ Work Start Date cannot be changed after saving ‚Äî enter it accurately.

Tracking Job Retention
Retention 30, 90, and 180-day milestones
üìä
Performance Contract: Retention 30 is your second reimbursement milestone. Once a client reaches 30 days of employment and you mark it Complete in ECM, you can submit a Retention voucher for payment.

After a client is employed in ECM, a Follow-up target is automatically added to the IEP with three tasks: Retention 30, Retention 90, and Retention 180. Dates are auto-calculated from the Work Start Date.

Completing Retention Milestones

1
On the Progress Note tab, document client check-ins at or before each milestone

Note any employment updates, challenges, or changes in status.

2
Once the milestone date arrives: IEP tab ‚Üí Follow-up target ‚Üí edit pencil next to the task

3
Set Task Status to "Completed" ‚Üí Save

ECM will not allow Completed status before the milestone date.‚ö†Ô∏è After marking Retention 30 as Completed, proceed to Section 8 to submit your voucher.

Auto Payment Billing (Vouchers) Most Support Calls
Creating, submitting, and tracking voucher payments
üí≥
This section is critical. Auto Payment Billing is how your agency gets reimbursed under your performance-based contract. Errors or missed steps here are the most common cause of delayed or denied payments. Read every step carefully before submitting.

The Auto Payment page has two tabs: Auto Payment (your agency creates and submits vouchers) and Auto Reconciliation (the City approves or denies payments). You work exclusively in the Auto Payment tab.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Clients do not appear in the Billing List immediately. After a client is Enrolled or their Retention 30 task is marked Complete, the system takes up to one business day to process that record into the Auto Payment Billing list. If a client is not showing up, wait until the next business day before investigating further. If they are still missing after that, check their IEP status.

Creating a Voucher

1
Navigate to the Auto Payment Billing page from the left navigation

2
Click CREATE ‚Üí select your Agency ‚Üí click Search

All eligible clients (enrollment and Retention 30) appear in the Billing List.

3
Enter a clear, consistent Voucher Name

Recommended format: "Agency Name ‚Äì Month Year ‚Äì Type" (e.g., ABC Agency ‚Äì Feb 2025 ‚Äì Enrollment).

4
Use the checkboxes to select which clients to include

Only include clients whose ECM records are fully complete and verified.

5
Click Save and Close

The voucher appears on the Voucher List with a status of "New."

6
Review the Voucher List one final time, then click Submit

Status changes: New ‚Üí Pending.‚ö†Ô∏è Vouchers cannot be edited after submission. Review carefully before clicking Submit.

7
Print the Voucher Transmittal Form

Forms button (top right) ‚Üí Application Forms ‚Üí Application Report List ‚Üí Print next to Voucher Transmittal Form.

üö® Common Billing Issues & Troubleshooting

Client isn't appearing in my Billing List ‚ñæ

The client's WF Placement record is likely incomplete. Check these before contacting support:

‚Ä¢ Enrollment vouchers: Confirm the client has been Enrolled in the IEP tab (Enroll button clicked, enrollment date saved).

‚Ä¢ Retention 30 vouchers: Confirm Retention 30 Task Status is set to "Completed" and saved under IEP ‚Üí Follow-up target.

‚Ä¢ Confirm the application is Verified ‚Äî HH Members tab lock icon should be closed.

My voucher was Denied ‚Äî what do I do? ‚ñæ

Click History next to the voucher to find the denial reason. Common causes:

‚Ä¢ Missing or unverified documentation on the application

‚Ä¢ Client was not fully exited from a previous agency before your enrollment

‚Ä¢ Retention milestone date had not been reached at time of submission

Correct the issue in the client's record first, then contact ECM Technical Support: ecm_support@wegrp.com | (844) 726-2999.

I submitted a voucher but need to make a change ‚ñæ

Submitted vouchers cannot be edited. Review your Billing List carefully before submitting.

If submitted in error, contact ECM Technical Support immediately: ecm_support@wegrp.com | (844) 726-2999.

I can't find a previous voucher ‚ñæ

Use the AutoPayment Voucher Quick Access menu. Select your Agency, select a Status filter (New, Pending, Approved, or Denied), and click Search. Click any result to open it on the Auto Payment tab.

How do I print the Voucher Transmittal Form? ‚ñæ

After submitting: click Forms at the top right ‚Üí click "Application Forms" ‚Üí in the Application Report List, click Print next to Voucher Transmittal Form.

Retention 30 task won't save as Completed ‚ñæ

ECM blocks Completed status until the milestone date has passed. Check the Target Date on the Retention 30 task row ‚Äî you must wait until on or after that date.

If the date has clearly passed and the issue persists, verify the Work Start Date entered during the Employ step is correct. Contact ECM Technical Support if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions from CRSC staff and Workforce Agencies
My client isn't appearing in the Billing List ‚Äî what do I check? ‚ñæ

The most common reason is that the client's WF Placement record isn't fully complete yet. Check the following before anything else:

‚Ä¢ Enrollment vouchers: Confirm the client has been Enrolled on the IEP tab (Enroll button clicked and enrollment date saved).

‚Ä¢ Retention 30 vouchers: Confirm the Retention 30 task status is set to "Completed" under IEP ‚Üí Follow-up target, and that the milestone date has passed.

‚Ä¢ Timing: The system takes up to one business day to reflect enrollment or Retention 30 completions in the Billing List. If everything looks correct, wait until the next business day before investigating further.

‚Ä¢ Confirm the application is verified ‚Äî the lock icon on the HH Members tab should be closed.

I have a billing question or my voucher was denied ‚Äî who do I contact? ‚ñæ

For billing questions, denied vouchers, or payment status issues, contact your DFSS Program Manager directly. They are your primary point of contact for all billing and reimbursement matters under your contract.

For technical issues with the ECM system itself (login problems, system errors, data not saving), contact ECM Technical Support:

üìß ecm_support@wegrp.com  |  üìû (844) 726-2999

I submitted a voucher but need to make a change ‚Äî can I edit it? ‚ñæ

Submitted vouchers cannot be edited. Always review your Billing List carefully before clicking Submit. If you submitted a voucher in error, contact your DFSS Program Manager immediately.

A client is active with another agency and I can't complete intake ‚Äî what do I do? ‚ñæ

ECM will block intake if the client is currently active with another Workforce agency. Contact DFSS to request that the client be exited from the other agency. Once the exit is processed, return to the application and click Verify Check to refresh the Verify List and continue.

Retention 30 won't let me set the task to Completed ‚Äî why? ‚ñæ

ECM blocks the Completed status until the milestone date has been reached. Check the Target Date on the Retention 30 task row ‚Äî you must wait until on or after that date to mark it complete. The target date is calculated automatically from the client's Work Start Date entered when they were employed.

If the date has clearly passed and the issue continues, verify the Work Start Date is correct. Contact ECM Technical Support if the problem persists.

Workforce agencies aren't appearing in the referral pop-up ‚Äî what's wrong? ‚ñæ

The agency list is populated based on the client's address. If no agencies appear, one of the following is likely the issue:

‚Ä¢ The client's address was not entered on the Encounter form before saving

‚Ä¢ The address entered is not a valid Chicago address

Return to the Encounter record, update the address to a valid Chicago address, save, then try the referral again.

I see a duplicate client record ‚Äî what should I do? ‚ñæ

Do not continue adding data to a duplicate record. Contact your ECM Administrator or DFSS Program Manager to report the duplicate. They will coordinate the merge or cleanup. Going forward, always search the Account Summary page thoroughly before creating any new account to prevent duplicates.
