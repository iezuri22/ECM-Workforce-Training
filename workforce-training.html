System Overview

The Enterprise Content Management (ECM) system is the City of Chicago's centralized platform for tracking client services across multiple departments. For the Workforce Division, ECM serves as the primary tool for managing workforce development program participants from intake through case closure.

ECM is accessed through a web browser ‚Äî no software installation required. Always use your assigned City of Chicago credentials to log in. Do not share login credentials.

  • Service encounter documentation

  • Reporting for federal and city compliance requirements

Searching & Deduplication
‚ñæ

Before creating any new client record, you must always search for an existing client first. Duplicate records create reporting errors and can negatively impact a client's service history. The deduplication process is one of the most critical steps in ECM data quality.

‚úÖ
Best Practice: Search by at least two identifiers (e.g., last name + date of birth) to reduce false negatives. A client may be in the system under a slightly different spelling.

How to Search for a Client

  • Navigate to the Client Search panel from the main menu
  • Enter the client's last name and date of birth at minimum
  • Review all results carefully before concluding no match exists
  • If a match is found, open that record ‚Äî do not create a new one
  • If no match is found after thorough searching, proceed to create a new record

Creating a New Application
‚ñæ

Once you have confirmed the client does not already exist in ECM, you can create a new application. This establishes their official program record within the Workforce Division.

Required Fields

  • Legal first and last name
  • Date of birth
  • Social Security Number (last 4 digits minimum)
  • Address and contact information
  • Program type and funding source
  • Referral source
‚ÑπÔ∏è
Fields marked with a red asterisk (*) are required and must be completed before saving. Saving an incomplete record may result in the application being flagged during QA review.

Recording Encounters
‚ñæ

Every meaningful interaction with a client should be documented as an encounter in ECM. Encounters are the primary way the Workforce Division tracks service delivery and demonstrates program activity for reporting and compliance.

Types of Encounters

  • In-person meeting ‚Äî face-to-face sessions with the client
  • Phone contact ‚Äî outreach or follow-up calls
  • Workshop / group session ‚Äî attendance at a program-run event
  • Job placement ‚Äî recording a successful placement outcome
  • Follow-up ‚Äî post-placement check-ins within required timeframes
‚úÖ
Encounters should be entered the same day they occur whenever possible. Backdating should be avoided unless you have supervisor approval and a documented reason.
